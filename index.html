Larissa Barra is online

Me in a nutshell

I'm a Brazilian living in London since early 2020. I have moved countries because of work, but this move turned out to be a great personal experience - after all, we all know what happened in 2020.

Sustainability is one of my main passions, as is all that comes with it (including endless questioning of life, habits and beliefs that I was brought up with). I enjoy trying to fix before buying anew, and can frequently be found considering ways of generating a smaller footprint.

I'm also into arts and crafts, going for hikes and socialising, circumstances permitting.

Yes, I do love coding, but from 9 to 5 (preferably for 4 days a week only). Although I am a software engineer in occupation, I refuse to use that title to describe myself as a person, and I believe this reveals a lot about myself as a professional.

I will strive for a good work-life balance, both for me and for any team I am part of. That involves negotiating and setting expectations with whoever is on the business side of a software project: clients, external or internal, product owners, project managers. That also involves working hard on developer experience and on the team's productivity, providing testing and delivery infrastructure, working on code quality and good architecture and design. Finally, users must be happy, so their app must be monitored and they must be understood. Count on me for putting my best effort into that: I believe in coding for people, not only for the machines.

In the end, 2020 wasn't so bad. It's helped me set some priorities straight. We could barely separate personal and professional contexts, and that's why I believe in being tested and hired for what I bring to the table as Larissa, not just as "an engineer".

Me, an engineer

Since I was quite young, I knew I was going to be in STEM. At the age of 8 I used to say I was going to be a Mathematician (oh, sweet child), then decided to change my degree to Physics at around 10 (I really didn't know what was waiting for me). At 12 I had embraced an artistic side and wanted to be an Architect, not knowing that I would end up becoming one - just not one that designs buildings.
